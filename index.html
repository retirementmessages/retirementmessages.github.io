What to Say in a Retirement Card

Not being confident about writing a retirement card message is common. You might be unsure if the best words to use or finding it difficult to express exactly how you feel about whoever is retiring.

  • Enjoy your retirement, you've earned it!
  • Wishing you a truly happy retirement
  • Happy retirement - just don't forget the rest of us!
  • Best wishes and good luck for a fulfilling retirement
  • Enjoy this new journey!
  • Wishing you a lengthy and satisfying retirement
  • Wishing you well through retirement!
  • Wishing you the best in this next phase in your life
  • Enjoy your golden years!
  • Wishing you a long and joyous retirement

What NOT to say in a Retirement Card

There a few things you should avoid saying in your retirement message. The last thing you want to do is upset the retiree and some of these phrases or words could do so.

  • Don't be negative. Stay upbeat and use encouraging, positive phrases and words to make them feel more confident
  • Don't mention death! This should be obvious but retirement should mean a new beginning, and is nothing to do with dying
  • Don't use anything religious in your message unless you are confident they will appreciate it. If you're in any doubt then just stick with a regular message
  • Don't act or talk differently. Retirement is just finishing work, nothing more, so they will want you to be as normal as possible with them. So write and talk as you usually would to them
